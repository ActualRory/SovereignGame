# Rulers RPG — N.O. Reference

---

## Core Structure

- **Players:** 3–6 Rulers + 1 Narrative Overlord (N.O.)
- **Turn = Season.** 4 seasons = 1 year.
- **Each turn:** Players submit a **Report** containing Financial Actions, Political Actions (max 6 action words), and the in-game date.
- **Dice:** d6 pools. **5–6 = success.** Opposed rolls: most successes wins.

## Trade

- Two realms sign Trade Agreement → **Trade Route.**
- Each exported product: **+5% wealth** to connected Settlement.
- Partner gains access to each other's Resources for building/recruitment at **+25% cost.**
- Products trade as monopolies; best quality wins; tie → lowest tax rate wins.
- Changed product protected for **2 seasons.**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Village | 4 | Medium | 6 | 4 | 1 |
| Town | 6 | Large | 10 | 6 | 2 |

- Food production: **1 Food per empty building slot** in settlement.

### Food Shortage Effects

| Duration | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 season | Narrative unrest only |
| 2+ seasons | Turmoil +1/4 current value (rounds up), rising to +1/2 each successive season |

- Turmoil from shortage persists until **2 full seasons** of adequate food.

## Warfare

### Level 1 — Automatic

No standing military. Army moves into tile. If an army moves into an enemy-controlled hex, they can do the following:

Liberation makes the hex neutral.
Raze destroys all buildings and most of the pop.
Capture places it under the control of the conquering nation.

### Level 2 — Combat

Every minor turn two armies at in the same tile, this happens:
- Basic Troop: 1d6, success = 5–6. Elite Troop: 1d6, success = 4–6.
- +1 die per 3 units of same type. Bonuses applied once per type (same bonus stacks).
- If both sides exceed 10 dice, roll only the difference. This way battles can take multiple turns.
- Siege: defender's pool += settlement/fortification defence rating.

**Settlement Defence Ratings (Quick Combat):** Village 0 | Town 2 | City 4 | Metropolis 6 |  +Walls +1 | +Fort +2 | +Castle +4


### Advantage Table (net successes)

| Net Successes | Result |
|---|---|
| +1–2 | Wounded |
| +3–4 | Routed |
| +5 | Defeated |
| +6+ | Crushed (removed from game entirely) |

### Wound Track

Wounded 1: −1 die → Wounded 2: −1 die → Routed 1: −2 dice → Routed 2: −2 dice → Defeated: out.

**Routed units:** make Morale save (base dice pool vs successes rolled against them, target 3 or 4).
- Pass: stay, −2 dice on future rolls.
- Fail: flee (effectively Defeated).
- 3rd failed Morale = auto Defeated.

### Siege Weapons in Full Combat

- Requires Load action then Fire action (not same turn). Trebuchet/Cannon cannot move.
- Roll combat + damage bonus vs fortification defence pool. Each net success = remove 1 die from fort pool.
- Fort at half dice = Damaged. Fort at 0 dice = Destroyed (troops on it = Defeated).
- Deflected projectile: roll 1d10 — 1–3 over, 4–6 short, 7–8 on target (no damage), 9–10 random battlefield unit.

### Other Warfare Rules

- **Blockade:** Army surrounds settlement — stops trade, food, resources. Lasts until army leaves or is defeated.
- **Capture:** Resolved combat → settlement joins attacker's realm. Raises Turmoil.
- **Prisoners:** Defeated troops + nobles. Cost 1/4 upkeep to feed. Can release, execute, or ransom.

## Political Actions

- Max **6 action words** per report.
- Cannot fabricate from nothing or abruptly destroy/alter things.
- Can be assigned to Noble Agents (costs 1 action word slot).
- Can include trigger conditions.

**35 Action Words:** Accuse, Arrange, Attack, Ban, Begin, Capture, Close, Condemn, Convert, Declare, Defend, Deliver, Discuss, Draft, End, Expel, Explore, Give, Guard, Hold, Imprison, Intercept, Investigate, Invite, Move, Negotiate, Offer, Open, Pardon, Propose, Remove, Request, Search, Send, Take.

## Ruler & Realm Death

- **Ruler death:** Heir takes over. No heir → player controls a significant NPC. New ruler decided by N.O.
- **Realm death:** All settlements captured. Player keeps 10% treasury; most loyal nobles may remain. Player may become a noble under another ruler.

## Character Creation Checklist

1. Choose Resources
2. Choose Government type
3. Choose 3 Traditions
4. Create Ruler (Name, Race, Family, Backstory, Personality)
5. Roll Noble Families (1d4 families, 1d6 members each)
6. Assemble Starting Army
7. Create GOS
8. Define Realm (race, faith, language, culture)

**Noble family generation:** 1d10 for sex (1–5 male, 6–10 female). Age: 1 = Infant, 2–3 = Adolescent, 4–8 = Adult, 9–10 = Elderly. Adults roll 1d4 children. Personality: roll 1d10 each for trait, ruler relationship, who they value, object they value, core belief, desire.

## World Creation (N.O.)

1. Roll 8 climates (2d10) + location (1d8): Desert 2–3 | Savannah 4–6 | Mediterranean 7–8 | Steppes 9–10 | Oceanic 11 | Forested 12–13 | Alpine 14–15 | Tropical 16–18 | Tundra 19–20
2. Roll 1d3+1 mountain ranges with locations.
3. Roll 6 water features (1d10): Lake 1 | River 2–4 | Estuary 5–6 | Coast 7–9 | Island 10
4. Sketch: coastlines → mountains → rivers → climates → colour.

**Territories:** 1 per player + 1 per 2 players (NPC realm) + 1 per 2 players (neutral).

**Resources per player realm:** 3 Common (1d10: Timber 1–3, Clay 4–6, Iron 7–8, Stone 9, Luxury 10) + 1 Luxury (1d10: Silver 1–2, Porcelain 3–4, Lacquer 5–6, Jewels 7, Cotton 8, Marble 9, Expanded 10).
